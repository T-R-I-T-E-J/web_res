Introduction

Para Shooting was founded in 1951 with the view to promote and popularize the shooting sports in India. Shri G.V. Mavlankar was the founder President and Shri M.A.S. Iyengar was the founder Secretary of the Association.

Para Shooting is the apex body for shooting sports in India and is affiliated to the International Shooting Sport Federation (ISSF), Asian Shooting Confederation, Commonwealth Shooting Federation, and the Indian Olympic Association.

History

Over the decades, shooting has emerged as one of the most successful sports for India at the international level. From Rajyavardhan Singh Rathore's silver at Athens 2004 to Abhinav Bindra's historic gold at Beijing 2008, Indian shooters have consistently brought laurels to the country.

Para Shooting has played a pivotal role in this journey by providing world-class infrastructure, training, and exposure to Indian athletes.
